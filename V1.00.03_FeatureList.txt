# 🪶 **Smollyfe Major Update Design Report — “The Living Life Patch”**

---

## **Overview**

This update transforms *Smollyfe* from a sandbox of stats into a **living simulation of ambition, emotion, and consequence**.
The systems now breathe together — careers, family, relationships, fame, stress, and random events create an organic, replayable world where every life feels unique and narratively rich.

The goal: **immersion through cohesion**.
No single feature stands alone; everything loops back into relationships, milestones, and story arcs that feel *alive*.

---

## **Core Additions & Systems Integration**

### **1. Occupation System Overhaul**

#### **Core Changes**

* **15 Expanded Careers**, each with dynamic work hours, stress scaling, and unique story hooks.
* **9–5 Jobs (40 hrs)** standardized: stress only increases if additional jobs or activities exceed 40 hrs/week.
* **Career branching:** promotions, burnout events, lateral transitions, and unique milestones for each profession.

#### **Career Highlights**

| Career                                      | Key Features                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Software Engineer**                       | Can evolve into *Startup Founder*; workload affects stress; innovation events.                                                                                                            |
| **Teacher / Professor**                     | Stable 9–5 with mentorship arcs and family-life synergy.                                                                                                                                  |
| **Doctor / Surgeon**                        | High stress and income; rare life-or-death events; malpractice or breakthrough stories.                                                                                                   |
| **Police → Detective**                      | New *Case Progression System*: overtime work increases progress, income, and stress. Detectives uncover mafia ties, political corruption, or cold cases that cross into other storylines. |
| **Politician (Ethical/Corrupt)**            | Optional fame; bribery, campaigns, and public scandal paths.                                                                                                                              |
| **Mafia (Russian/Italian)**                 | High-risk/high-reward with underworld fame, betrayals, and moral degradation arcs.                                                                                                        |
| **NBA/NFL Athlete**                         | Auto-fame with managers, endorsements, injury risks, and legacy options (coach/commentator).                                                                                              |
| **Influencer / Streamer / Singer / Rapper** | Fame-driven lifestyles with manager systems, burnout events, and scandals.                                                                                                                |
| **Author / Journalist / Founder**           | Low-hour, high-impact professions allowing introspective or media-rich arcs.                                                                                                              |

#### **Stress & Workload Rules**

* ≤40 hrs/week → baseline stress.
* > 40 hrs/week → compounding stress, risk of burnout, and health events.
* Certain careers (Doctor, Mafia, Athlete, Detective) inherently exceed 40 hrs baseline, raising tension and narrative pace.

---

### **2. Manager & Fame Action System**

#### **Eligibility**

* Automatically unlocks when Fame crosses a threshold or specific career flags are met (e.g., Athlete, Influencer, Singer, Famous Author).

#### **Manager Tiers**

| Tier     | Quality | Cut    | Effect                                                  |
| -------- | ------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Budget   | Low     | 5%     | Infrequent gigs, mishandled PR, minor missteps.         |
| Standard | Mid     | 10%    | Consistent, balanced career growth.                     |
| Elite    | High    | 15–20% | Major deals, crisis control, and high-tier appearances. |

#### **Famous Actions (Opportunities)**

Managers bring career-related events that build stress and Fame dynamically:

* TV guest appearances
* Brand endorsements
* Commercials & photo shoots
* Reality show cameos
* Movie auditions
* Magazine features
* Music collaborations
* Public speaking events
* Charity galas & social media campaigns
  Each opportunity can bring money, fame, stress, or reputation risks.

#### **Public Fame Thresholds**

| Fame Tier  | Effect                                                        |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **0–30**   | Private life; personal events only.                           |
| **30–60**  | Paparazzi begin noticing; gossip coverage starts.             |
| **60–80**  | Public scandals possible; fame actions frequent.              |
| **80–100** | Global celebrity; fan behavior, stalkers, mental toll events. |

---

### **3. Detective Case Progression System**

#### **Overview**

Detectives now maintain active investigations with **progress bars (0–100%)**.

#### **Case Types**

* Mafia
* Cartel
* Political Corruption
* Corporate Fraud
* Cold Case
* “Nothing Found” (burnout cases)

#### **Mechanics**

* **Overtime Hours:** Player can work extra (+10–20 hrs/week) → faster progress, bonus pay, higher stress, higher chance of uncovering corruption.
* **Outcome Events:** Arrests, promotions, revenge attempts, or moral dilemmas.
* **Crossovers:** Cases can directly intersect with Mafia or Politician storylines, merging narratives dynamically.

---

### **4. Relationship & Social Web System**

#### **Nodes & Bonds**

Every known person is tracked as a **node**:

* **Bond (0–100):** affection or trust level.
* **Type:** Family, Romantic, Friend, Rival, Professional.
* **Influence:** how much they impact your life.
* **History Thread:** contextual memories (“We met in college,” “She leaked my album”).

#### **Dynamic Interactions**

* Relationships fluctuate with stress, fame, neglect, and shared milestones.
* Emotional context changes yearly—friends drift, spouses cheat, children rebel, rivals reappear.

#### **Romantic Lifecycle**

| Stage        | Common Events      | Outcomes                      |
| ------------ | ------------------ | ----------------------------- |
| Flirtation   | texting, gifts     | relationship start or fade    |
| Relationship | arguments, bonding | marriage or breakup           |
| Marriage     | family growth      | children, prenup, affairs     |
| Affair       | secrecy, scandal   | divorce or public fallout     |
| Divorce      | custody, finances  | independence or regret arc    |
| Reconnection | nostalgia          | forgiveness or repeat mistake |

#### **Family Evolution**

* Parents age and face illness or pride arcs.
* Siblings form rivalry or support networks.
* Children inherit stats and can become playable heirs (Legacy Mode).

---

### **5. Fame, Scandals, and Public Image**

* Fame now **warps relationships and career events**:

  * Public meltdowns, leaked messages, tabloids.
  * Manager interventions and PR spin choices.
* High fame increases “noise,” lowering bond stability.
* Fame feeds stress, creating burnout and addiction paths.

---

### **6. Addiction & Vice System**

* Triggered by stress thresholds or fame-related coping events.
* Covers: alcohol, stimulants, gambling, painkillers.
* Temporary benefits (stress relief, energy boost) → long-term declines (health, reputation).
* Rehab and relapse milestones alter tone and endings.

---

### **7. Random Life Event Engine (Expanded)**

Now interconnected with all systems:

* Generates global, career, or relationship-driven events every year.
* Examples:

  * “Sibling’s wedding.”
  * “Child expelled.”
  * “Car accident.”
  * “Lottery win.”
  * “Charity scandal.”
  * “Old flame writes a memoir.”

Each event reacts to your stats, fame, relationships, and stress state.

---

### **8. Milestone & Legacy System**

Every notable action creates a permanent milestone (displayed chronologically in the History Log):

* “Graduated University”
* “First Major Paycheck”
* “Marriage”
* “Divorce”
* “Public Scandal”
* “Child Born”
* “First Award”
* “Midlife Crisis”
* “Rehab Recovery”

Milestones affect:

* Reputation modifiers
* Future events
* Unlock conditions for careers and fame actions

**Legacy Mode**:
If the player has children, their traits combine parental stats + fame, opening next-generation playthroughs where the parent’s reputation shapes the child’s world.

## **Narrative Architecture — The Smollyfe Cohesion Loop**

```
Activities → Stats → Milestones → Occupation → Relationships → Events → History
        ↑                                                   ↓
        └────────────── Stress / Fame Feedback ─────────────┘
```

Every element cycles into another:

* **Activities** shape stats and habits.
* **Stats** open career and social options.
* **Careers** create relationships and fame.
* **Fame** spawns scandals, fame actions, and family tension.
* **Relationships** feed emotional depth, children, or heartbreak.
* **Random events** tie it all together, ensuring no two lives unfold the same.
* **Milestones** mark every era — childhood, youth, adulthood, legacy.

# 🧬 **Smollyfe – Dynamic Relationships, Family, and Career Ecosystem Design Report**

## **1. Dynamic Relationship Building**

Relationships are the emotional bloodstream of Smollyfe. They breathe, age, and sometimes implode based on time, stress, fame, and shared experience. Every NPC that matters—lover, friend, parent, rival, manager—has evolving *emotional variables*.

### **Core Attributes per NPC Node**

| Attribute                     | Description                                                             |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bond (0–100)**              | Current affection/trust. Decays naturally over time if ignored.         |
| **Attraction (0–100)**        | Chemistry potential. Can ignite romantic arcs or affairs.               |
| **Familiarity (years known)** | Deepens conversations/events. Older friends generate nostalgia scenes.  |
| **Influence Level**           | Determines ability to alter your stress, career, or fame.               |
| **Public Exposure Flag**      | Marks which relationships are visible to tabloids once you’re famous.   |
| **Morality Compatibility**    | Quietly affects whether the relationship thrives under corruption/fame. |

---

### **Dynamic Relationship Phases**

| Stage                   | Core Experience                                               | Example Events                                                                 |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Introduction**        | Meeting new characters through jobs, school, or fame.         | “You sit next to someone in orientation.” “A fan slides into your DMs.”        |
| **Bonding**             | Shared moments and dialogue deepen connection.                | “You both rant about your boss.” “Late-night studio session sparks chemistry.” |
| **Maintenance**         | Requires effort: calls, dates, gifts, presence.               | “You missed their birthday.” “They surprise you with concert tickets.”         |
| **Conflict**            | Arises from neglect, jealousy, ideology, or fame noise.       | “They accuse you of cheating.” “Manager clashes with spouse.”                  |
| **Crossroads**          | Player decision defines outcome.                              | Breakup, forgiveness, affair, ghosting.                                        |
| **Long-Term Evolution** | Couples can marry, have kids, separate, or grow old together. | “25th anniversary.” “Divorce court.” “Spouse passes away peacefully.”          |

---

### **Dynamic Triggers**

Relationships react to:

* **Stress:** high stress → irritability → fights.
* **Fame:** high fame → jealousy, opportunists, fake love, PR relationships.
* **Money:** imbalance creates resentment or gold-digging.
* **Moral Choices:** corruption, infidelity, greed alter loyalty.
* **Time:** absence decays bonds; proximity heals.

---

### **Romantic Complexity**

* **Prenups:** appear if wealth gap is large pre-marriage.
* **Pregnancy System:** chance based on relationship strength, age, and random events.
* **Affairs:** high attraction + low satisfaction + fame opportunity.
* **Divorce Outcomes:** financial split, custody, and social consequence.
* **Rekindling:** old flames can resurface years later as different people.

## **2. Family Relationship System**

Family gives emotional context—your origin, support system, and eventual legacy.

### **Core Family Roles**

| Role                 | Behavioral Tendencies               | Possible Arcs                                                            |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Parents**          | Pride, disappointment, protection.  | “Help with tuition,” “Fall ill,” “Disown you for scandal.”               |
| **Siblings**         | Rivalry, camaraderie.               | “Compete for inheritance,” “Sibling becomes addict,” “Co-found company.” |
| **Extended Family**  | Appear for money, favors, holidays. | “Aunt asks for loan,” “Uncle dies leaving will.”                         |
| **Spouse / Partner** | The most volatile family member.    | “Shared mortgage,” “Open relationship,” “Public divorce.”                |
| **Children**         | Blend of player traits.             | “Child wins award,” “Rebel against fame,” “Follow your career path.”     |

### **Family Evolution Timeline**

| Life Stage      | Family Dynamics                         |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **Childhood**   | Dependence, shaping early values.       |
| **Teen Years**  | Rebellion or mentorship.                |
| **Adulthood**   | Financial independence, changing roles. |
| **Parenthood**  | Role reversal; balancing work and kids. |
| **Elder Years** | Aging, regret, legacy building.         |

### **Emotional Mechanics**

* Family affection rises with visits, calls, gifts.
* Drops with neglect, fame scandals, political corruption, or criminal ties.
* Parents can forgive through “Redemption” milestones.
* Children reflect reputation: famous parents spawn curious or resentful kids.

## **5. Under-the-Hood Relationship Logic**

* Each year, every major relationship runs a *micro-simulation*:

  * +Bond for shared events, visits, or kindness.
  * −Bond for neglect, fame scandals, stress outbursts.
  * Random *event seeds* fire based on thresholds.
* When Bond <30, risk of breakup or estrangement event.
* When Bond >80, chance of marriage or joint milestone.
* Fame adds “Noise” modifier: high Fame accelerates both affection and decay.

## **7. Replayability Blueprint**

* **Interlocking randomness:** every system talks to others—no predictable loops.
* **Multiple moral spectrums:** good vs corrupt, loyal vs selfish, grounded vs fame-hungry.
* **Generational inheritance:** children embody your legacy, continuing stories.
* **Adaptive tone:** quiet lives feel cozy; famous lives spiral chaotically.
* **Branching love stories:** same characters can yield different fates depending on timing and stress.

## **8. Player Experience Goal**

> To make each life feel like an *autobiography you didn’t mean to write.*

Players don’t chase a victory screen; they chase emotional closure:

* *“Did my detective finally solve the cartel case?”*
* *“Did my singer rebuild her marriage after rehab?”*
* *“Did my child forgive me for the scandal?”*

The joy lies in **seeing the world react organically**, telling new stories each time.

## **9. Summary Vision**

Smollyfe now operates as a **self-contained narrative ecosystem**:

* Careers generate characters.

* Relationships generate drama.
* Drama shapes milestones.

Every variable—Intelligence, Attractiveness, Stress, Fame—ripples outward, ensuring no two lives ever align the same way.
Whether you live as a small-town teacher, a corrupt politician, or a fallen pop icon, the simulation remembers, reacts, and retells the story differently each time.
